Key Differences Between Mice and Rats in Southern Kentucky

Physical Characteristics

  • Mice: 2-4 inches long body, large ears, pointed snout, thin tail
  • Rats: 6-9 inches long body, smaller ears relative to head, blunt snout, thick tail

Behavior Patterns

  • Mice: Curious, will explore new objects
  • Rats: Cautious, avoid new objects initially
  • Both: Most active at dawn and dusk

Common Signs of Rodent Infestation in Bowling Green Homes

Whether you're in Downtown Bowling Green or Russellville, watch for these telltale signs:

  • Droppings along walls or in pantries
  • Gnaw marks on wood or wiring
  • Scratching sounds in walls or ceilings
  • Grease marks along baseboards

Health Risks and Property Damage

Both mice and rats can cause significant problems, similar to issues we discuss in our guide about identifying unwanted guests in your attic. These include:

  • Disease transmission through droppings
  • Contamination of food sources
  • Electrical fires from chewed wiring
  • Structural damage to insulation and drywall

Prevention is crucial for long-term rodent control:
